This novel is a work of very considerable merit. 
It is written with a degree of elegance and precision which has seldom been surpassed, 
and it displays a knowledge of human nature, and particularly of female character, 
which is truly admirable.

The story is simple, but it is conducted with great skill, and the interest is 
well sustained throughout. The characters are drawn with much spirit and 
discrimination; and though some of them may be thought a little overcharged, 
they are all consistent, and contribute to the general effect.

The heroine is particularly well conceived; she is lively, 
intelligent, and possesses a strong sense of propriety, though she is sometimes 
led astray by prejudice and hastiness of judgment. The man, on the other hand, 
though at first disagreeable from his pride, gradually wins the reader’s esteem 
by the integrity of his character and the sincerity of his attachment.

The dialogue is easy and natural, and frequently distinguished by a quiet wit 
which gives great animation to the narrative. The author has also succeeded 
in exposing the follies and absurdities of certain classes of society without 
resorting to harshness or exaggeration.

There are, however, some defects. The incidents are occasionally somewhat 
improbable, and the conduct of certain minor characters might have been more 
fully developed. Yet these are slight blemishes in a work which, upon the whole, 
must be considered as one of the most pleasing productions of modern fiction.

We hesitate not to recommend this novel to our readers as a work of uncommon 
excellence, combining amusement with instruction in a very agreeable manner.
